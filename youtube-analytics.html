YouTube Viewer Analytics

Personal viewing analytics feature that allows YouTube users to track their own watching habits, preferences, and viewing statistics

Duration: 4 weeks
Role: UX/UI Designer
Tools: Figma, Data Visualization, User Research
Personal Analytics Data Visualization Behavioral Insights

Project Overview

I realized that I spend hours watching YouTube but have no idea what my actual habits look like. Do I binge-watch cooking videos at night? Am I slowly becoming obsessed with true crime? This project creates a personal analytics dashboard that finally shows viewers their own patterns - like Spotify Wrapped, but for YouTube.

The Problem

YouTube knows everything about what we watch, but we know almost nothing about our own viewing habits. How much time do we actually spend? What are we really into? When do we watch the most?

The Solution

A dashboard that takes all that data YouTube already has and turns it into something actually useful for viewers. Time breakdowns, genre deep-dives, and those "aha!" moments when you realize you've been watching 3 hours of productivity videos instead of actually being productive.

Design Process

1. User Research

I talked to people about their YouTube habits - turns out everyone is secretly curious about their own viewing patterns but nobody knows where to find that info.

2. Data Mapping

Figured out what data YouTube already tracks and how to make it actually interesting instead of just overwhelming numbers.

3. Information Architecture

Broke down all that data into bite-sized pieces: how much time you spend, what you're obsessed with, and those random rabbit holes you fall into.

4. Wireframing & Prototyping

Made dashboard mockups that turn boring stats into something you'd actually want to scroll through and share with friends.

5. Visual Design

Made the data actually fun to look at - because nobody wants to stare at boring spreadsheets of their own procrastination habits.

Key Features

Your Viewing Time Dashboard

See exactly how many hours you've spent watching (prepare to be shocked), when you watch the most, and those impressive viewing streaks you didn't even know you had.

What You're Really Into

Finally understand if you're a cooking video person, a true crime addict, or secretly obsessed with videos about organizing. Plus get recommendations that actually make sense.

Your Viewing Patterns

Discover when you're most likely to fall into a YouTube hole, how your habits change throughout the week, and whether you're a "watch one video" or "suddenly it's 3am" person.

Results & Impact

This feature concept gives YouTube viewers valuable self-awareness about their consumption habits while creating opportunities for better content discovery and platform engagement. The design transforms passive viewing data into engaging personal insights.

User Benefits

  • Self-awareness about viewing habits and time management
  • Discovery of viewing patterns they weren't conscious of
  • Improved content recommendations based on detailed preference analysis
  • Shareable viewing statistics similar to Spotify Wrapped
  • Motivation to explore new content categories

What I Learned

  • Personal data can be really interesting when you present it right. Nobody wants spreadsheets of their procrastination
  • People love seeing their own patterns, especially when it's packaged like Spotify Wrapped
  • The key is making data feel like discovery, not judgment
  • Sometimes the most useful insights come from data people didn't even know existed
